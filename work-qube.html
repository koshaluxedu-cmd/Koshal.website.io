Theatre Greeting Experience

— Designing a scalable B2C theatre greeting platform that balances delight, trust, and operational constraints.

Industry
Cinema / Entertainment
Role
UX Strategy & Design
Platform
Mobile-first B2C
Focus
Trust & conversion

— Business goal
01

The opportunity.

Theatre screens have underutilized greeting and ad slots, while users lack a simple way to create personalized big-screen greeting experiences.

The aim was to build a seamless greeting booking experience that increases slot utilization for theatres and creates memorable moments for users — without trading one off against the other.

North Star Metric
Greeting Slot Occupancy Rate
The percentage of available greeting slots booked across theatre screens per show. It reflects:
  • User adoption
  • Repeat usage
  • Theatre inventory utilization
  • Overall product success
Guardrail Metric
NPS — Net Promoter Score
User satisfaction and emotional sentiment after the greeting experience. It ensures occupancy growth never comes at the cost of:
  • User trust
  • Greeting quality
  • Moderation experience
  • Overall delight
Supporting Metrics
The signals beneath
  • Greeting creation completion time
  • % of content rejection
  • Average slot duration booked
  • Time to book
  • Drop-off rate across screens
— Scope & assumptions
02

The boundaries.

The scope focused on designing a mobile-first B2C product that enables users to create and book personalized theatre greetings, displayed during pre-show or intermission slots.

01Product

  • Exists as a standalone mobile-first platform with web support
  • Ticket booking platforms can pass booking details through deep-link integrations
  • Users primarily access the experience through mobile devices

02Operational

  • Showtimes and greeting slots are available up to 7 days in advance
  • Each show has limited greeting inventory across pre-show and intermission slots
  • Greetings go through a moderation review before approval
  • Moderation completes within 24–48 hours before playback

03User

  • Primary audience: users aged 18–35
  • Users plan greetings 2–7 days in advance for occasions like birthdays and anniversaries
— Research
03

How I learned.

I ran one-on-one depth interviews with users who frequently book movie tickets and plan surprise experiences — probing booking behaviour, trust concerns, personalization expectations, payment hesitation, and mobile usability challenges.

Alongside, I ran iterative flow exploration — multiple flow iterations and usability reviews refining theatre discovery, slot selection, the personalization flow, preview-before-payment, and moderation visibility.

Primary persona

In-Theatre Surprise Planner

Users already attending the movie who want to surprise friends, partners, or family during the pre-show or intermission. Prioritized as primary based on user research.

Goals
  • Create a memorable shared moment
  • Quickly personalize a greeting
  • Ensure the greeting plays successfully during the movie
Key expectations
  • Fast setup
  • Easy personalization
  • Playback confidence
  • Smooth mobile experience
Secondary persona

Remote Surprise Planner

Users booking greetings remotely for someone else who is attending the movie.

Goals
  • Coordinate a surprise from a different location
  • Ensure accurate theatre and show selection
  • Receive confirmation and status visibility
Key expectations
  • Clear moderation updates
  • Reliable playback communication
  • Simple theatre and slot confirmation
  • Trust before and after payment
— Reframing the problem
04

It was about confidence, not booking.

The challenge was not just about creating a greeting booking flow — it was about building user confidence throughout the experience. Users needed reassurance at four distinct moments.

01Slot selection

  • Users worried about accidentally booking the greeting slot for the wrong movie, show, or screen.

02Templates

  • Users felt available templates might restrict creativity and result in greetings that look repetitive or outdated.

03Payment

  • Users were hesitant to pay until confident about the overall experience — and worried about refunds if content wasn't approved.

04Moderation

  • Users were uncertain about the moderation process due to limited transparency around what's allowed or prohibited.
— The key decision
05

Pay first, or create first?

The single most consequential flow decision: should users pay to lock the slot before creating, or create and preview before committing payment?

I weighed both against the guardrail — user trust — and the north star — slot occupancy.

Payment first
Create greeting later
Strengths
  • Slot security — slot locked immediately, no risk of losing it mid-creation
  • User flexibility — create anytime within a day of the show, no time pressure
  • High conversion — commitment comes before emotional attachment to creation
  • Business certainty — revenue locked, fewer cancellations
Risks
  • Payment anxiety — paying ₹900 without seeing the result hurts trust
  • Editing friction — duration mismatches force awkward upgrade prompts
  • Abandonment — "I'll create it later" often becomes never
  • Low engagement — disconnected from the exciting creation moment
Create first Chosen
Payment later
Strengths
  • User confidence — create, preview, then commit. Zero regret risk
  • Engagement peak — payment lands at the moment of highest excitement
  • Natural flow — matches the mental model: create something, then pay for it
Risks (and how I mitigated them)
  • Payment drop-off — addressed with preview-first momentum into checkout
  • Longer funnel — offset with deep-link autofill and AI-assisted creation
  • Slot taken mid-creation — softened with short slot holds and live availability

— Pain points & solution design
07
01

Re-entering movie & show details manually

Users already booking through ticketing platforms didn't want to search and re-enter theatre, movie, and showtime details again.

Solution
  • Booking-link parsing to auto-fetch movie and show details
  • Ticket screenshot upload as a fallback recovery option
  • Auto-filled booking confirmation screen for quick validation
[ Onboarding / "How would you like to continue" screen ]
02

Too many templates made it hard to choose

Users felt overwhelmed and worried that templates would feel repetitive or outdated.

Solution
  • Curated categories — birthdays, cinematic, trending
  • Filters for occasion, duration, and style
  • Preview-first browsing for easier decision-making
[ Template filter / browse screen ]
03

Customization needed faster personalization

The existing customization flow asked too much effort for users who wanted something quick and polished.

Solution
  • AI-assisted greeting creation from a photo and a prompt
  • Preview-first customization experience
[ AI creation / "Create your own template with AI" screen ]
04

Worry about approval after payment

Users feared paying and then having their greeting rejected, with no visibility into what happens next.

Solution
  • Content guidelines shown during customization
  • Moderation and approval status tracking after booking
[ Booking confirmation / status tracker screen ]
— Handling content moderation
08

Trust, at every phase.

Moderation was the biggest source of post-payment anxiety. I designed transparency into three distinct phases of the journey so users always knew where they stood.

During creation
Set expectations early
  • Content guidelines shown contextually during customization
  • Inline warnings flag restricted content — profanity, nudity, offensive or copyrighted material
  • Helps users understand moderation expectations before finishing
Before payment
No surprises at checkout
  • A "Ready for Review" section explains that greetings go through moderation before playback
  • Clear messaging on what may not be approved, set before money changes hands
After payment
Visible progress
  • Status tracker: Submitted → Under Review → Playing at the Theatre
  • Confirmation and progress updates throughout the review process
"

The real challenge wasn't building a booking flow — it was earning confidence at every step, from slot to screen.

— Design principle for the project
