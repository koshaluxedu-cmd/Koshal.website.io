— Process · 03 / 05
— Five movements

Five movements I return to on every project — flexible, never rigid, shaped by the specific people I'm designing with and for.

01

Listen.

Interviews, ethnographic research, and a lot of patient observation. I want to understand the texture of the problem before I touch a pixel.

↘
02

Frame.

Translating research into clear problem statements, opportunity areas, and a measurable definition of what good looks like.

↘
03

Sketch.

Low-fidelity, high-volume. Many bad ideas before any good ones. Paper, whiteboards, and quick Figma flows to think with my hands.

↘
04

Prototype.

Building interactive flows that feel real enough to test. The goal is to fail in private so the product can succeed in public.

↘
05

Refine.

Working alongside engineers through ship — tuning motion, accessibility, and edge cases until the experience feels considered all the way down.

↘
